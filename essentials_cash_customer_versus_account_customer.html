Cash Customer versus Account Customer

This article explores the differences between cash customers and account customers, and how to manage each type in the system.

Topics included in this article

  • Defining cash and account customers
  • Managing transactions for cash customers
  • Handling account customer transactions
  • Tracking and reporting for different customer types

Defining cash and account customers

Cash customers pay for goods or services at the time of purchase, while account customers have an established account with credit terms.

Managing transactions for cash customers

Transactions with cash customers are typically straightforward and require immediate payment.

  1. Record cash transactions at the point of sale.
  2. Ensure all cash payments are accurately accounted for and securely handled.
  3. Provide immediate receipts or proof of purchase to cash customers.

Handling account customer transactions

Account customers may have different payment terms and require more complex transaction management.

  1. Set up and manage customer accounts with specific credit terms in the system.
  2. Track purchases and payments for account customers over time.
  3. Send invoices or statements to account customers as per the agreed terms.

Tracking and reporting for different customer types

Use the system to track and generate reports on transactions for both cash and account customers.

  1. Utilize reporting features to analyze sales, payments, and outstanding balances.
  2. Segment reports by customer type for more detailed insights.
  3. Regularly review customer accounts for accuracy and up-to-date information.
