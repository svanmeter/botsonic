Availability within a Contract

 

 

Overview

 

This article discusses the different ways you can check for item availability from within a contract.

 

Topics covered in this article include:

 

  • Item availability
  • Category availability
  • Group availability
  • Package availability
  • Serialized item availability

 

From within a contract, there are several tools to view the availability for a particular item. Right-clicking on an item will produce a list of options.

 

Selecting the availability option will produce another list.

 

 

 

Item availability

 

This option shows the availability of the item on the contract for the week or month selected.

 

 

 

Category availability

 

This option shows the availability of all the items in the category that the item belongs to.
 

Note: The availability check for both category and group availability can be suspended by hitting the ‘Esc’ key while the check is in process.

 

 

 

Group availability

 

This option shows the availability of all of the items in the group that the item belongs to.

 

 

 

Package availability

 

This option shows the availability of all the components of an item’s package, if applicable. Otherwise, it just shows the availability of the item itself.

 

 

 

Serialized item availability

 

This option shows the availability of the other serialized items attached to a common header, if applicable.

 

 

Additionally, these screens have several different options in which to filter availability. The dates can be manipulated by either using the up and down arrows, typing the date into the box, or selecting the calendar. The user can also look at a company-wide level or store by store.

 

Along the bottom of the availability screens are three views: 1 Week, 4 Weeks Group Weekly, and 4 Weeks. These allow you to view availability either weekly or monthly, as well as group the weeks differently.
