Gmail Email Setup for Syrinx




Overview
An upcoming Google update will affect users who use Google as an email provider from being able to send emails from third-party apps like Point of Rental without proper setup. This article will show you how to configure and set up your Google email account to be used within your Syrinx software.

The new Google update will take effect on May 30, 2022. Google will no longer support the use of third-party apps or devices that require you to sign into your Google account using only your username and password.


 

Find your Email Credentials

Depending on how your email is set up in the program we are going to show you both the Global Settings and the User Specific Settings. 


Global Settings

Syrinx Configuration > System Options > General > Email.




 

User Email Setup

Syrinx SP v4 > User > User Options > Email




 

Set up an Application Password

To continue to use Gmail with third-party apps like Syrinx, Google will require you to use an App Password in place of your Google account password. The App Password is a 16-digit passcode that gives less secure apps or devices permission to access your Google account. App Passwords can only be used with accounts that have 2-Step Verification turned on. For more information on this please visit this Google support article here.
 
  1. Go to your Google Account.
  2. Select Security.
  3. Under "Signing into Google," select App Passwords. You may need to sign in. If you don’t have this option, it might be because:
    1. 2-Step Verification is not set up for your account.
    2. 2-Step Verification is only set up for security keys.
    3. Your account is through work, school, or other organizations.
    4. You turned on Advanced Protection.
  4. At the bottom, choose Select app and choose the app you're using > Select device and choose the device you’re using > Generate.
  5. Follow the instructions to enter the App Password. The App Password is the 16-character code in the yellow bar on your device.
  6. Tap Done.
Tip: Most of the time, you’ll only have to enter an App password once per app or device, so don’t worry about memorizing it.
