Digital Signature via Email

This article explains how digital signatures can be applied to documents through email, making it convenient for remote signing and document authentication.

Topics included in this article

  • Overview of digital signature via email
  • Steps to digitally sign a document via email
  • Security and authentication measures
  • Benefits and use cases

Overview of digital signature via email

Understand the concept of applying digital signatures to documents using email as the medium.

Steps to digitally sign a document via email

Learn the step-by-step process of digitally signing a document and sending it via email for authentication.

  1. Open the document in your email client.
  2. Select the digital signature option and follow the prompts to create your digital signature.
  3. Apply the digital signature to the document and send it securely.

Security and authentication measures

Explore the security features and measures in place to ensure the authenticity and integrity of digitally signed documents via email.

  1. Encryption and secure transmission of signed documents.
  2. Authentication of signatories to prevent unauthorized signatures.
  3. Verification processes for recipients.

Benefits and use cases

Discover the benefits and various scenarios where digital signatures via email are advantageous.

  1. Remote signing for geographically dispersed teams.
  2. Efficient document authentication for business and legal purposes.
  3. Reduced reliance on physical signatures and paperwork.
