INTERNAL | Miscellaneous Entries



In this article, we will give you basic information on how to locate and resolve a Miscellaneous Entry on a customer's computer. Finding, and more importantly, correcting Miscellaneous Entries can be tedious and time-consuming. It is important to know where to begin and understand the process of creating the Miscellaneous Entry.

Accounting Methods

One of the first steps in finding a Miscellaneous Entry is determining what kind of accounting method the customer is using (Open, Closed, or Cash). This is important because you need to understand what their money is to be accounted for. The type of accounting method they are using will determine how you will approach finding and resolving the Miscellaneous Entry.

Open Accrual - Income is reported when a contract is opened (whether it is paid or not) with the exception of reservations, which are reported when they are sent out. Under Open Accrual accounting, any income generated is instantly reported. This method can have substantial changes, including negative income days if contracts are opened for a long period but then closed at only a day’s rent.

Closed Accrual - Income is reported when a contract is closed (whether it is paid or not). This is the most common accounting method. No tax liability is generated until the contract is closed. This method does not have substantial changes as not many modifications are made to closed contracts.

Cash Accounting - Income is reported only when the entire contract is paid in full. Under Cash accounting, no sales or income tax is due until the contract is paid. The IRS usually does not allow companies that earn over a million dollars per year, or companies that have inventory on hand, to use Cash Accounting.


What can cause a Miscellaneous Entry?

Sometimes, searching for a Miscellaneous Entry can be like looking for a needle in a haystack. Is the Miscellaneous Entry one transaction for a set dollar amount or is it multiple transactions over multiple dollar amounts? Below we will discuss a few scenarios where a Miscellaneous Entry can occur.
 

Account Payment - If a payment is made for a store that has already closed, or if the payment is being taken while the End of Day runs, it can create a Miscellaneous Entry. Normally, this will correct itself the next day.

Transfers - A Miscellaneous Entry can be created when an item is transferred to another store but no record is created to receive the item. The original record goes to 0.00 but there is no balancing entry to adjust the inventory.


Note: The entry has to be deleted from the “Transferredbetweenstores” file and the G/L reran to correct the issue.


Parameters - If you have Save Mods on Cash Customers as Adjustments checked and you change the value of a contract (i.e., modify a Closed Cash contract with a deposit), this would result in a credit and a Miscellaneous Entry would be created. The Active Deposits report would show these credits.


Note: A Database Edit is needed to delete the deposit on credits. The Miscellaneous Entry would be counted that day.
 

Payments - Contracts are not paid but are posted into the cash drawer, resulting in a Miscellaneous Entry. Running the Trans Not Match report with Transhistory will usually show this entry.


Note: We will need a Database Edit to pay the contract so that the Miscellaneous Entry will show on the other side.


Register File - If someone modifies the register to make it match their End of Day count, it will create a Miscellaneous Entry.


Note: You can see this under Override reports >Transaction Edit History. You can also see this by running the Transaction Edit report under End of Day. It shows as “Register Modified.”


Work Orders - Payments taken on open Work Orders can sometimes cause a Miscellaneous Entry when the Work Order is closed.


Note: An open Work Order acts as a reservation. The payment should end up in the Suspense Fund but sometimes it does not.


Locks - Clearing an End of Day lock will generate a Miscellaneous Entry. You can confirm what lock was cleared by looking at the Error Log.


Interstore Balance - If the company is multi-store and uses Interstore Balance, the totals need to be equal from debits to credits. If they are not balanced, then that amount is a Miscellaneous Entry.

 

Contracts


Closed Contract - The contract is paid but it did not hit totals. The Transedit shows no payment and you are missing a modification. A Database Edit would be needed to increase the total by a penny and change the status to closed. Modifying the contract, lowering the value by a penny, and completing it will create a Miscellaneous Entry the other way.

Cash Contract - The contract is paid but did not hit totals. The Transedit shows no payment and you are missing a modification. A Database Edit would be needed to increase the total by a penny and change the status to closed. Modifying the contract, lowering the value by a penny, and completing it will create a Miscellaneous Entry the other way.

Open Contract - A Prompt Pay discount is taken on an open contract through Accounts Receivable. The discount was posted as a Miscellaneous Entry, as the income had not yet been posted. When the contract closes, the Miscellaneous Entry will be countered. You can run the Transaction Edits report, and the payments will show as Other Income.

Credit Contract - Somehow, a Credit contract was marked as completed and days later, the contract was sent to the Historical file, thus creating a Miscellaneous Entry. We will need to do a Database Edit and mark the credit as closed, so there will be an opposite entry.
 

Paid Contract - The contract is paid but the payment never hits the register. The Transaction Edit report in the contract shows blank before and after statuses on the payment line. The Trans Not Match report will show if the contract is active, otherwise, change to the Transhistory to see the historical view. You will need a Database Edit to pay the amount (the Miscellaneous Entry is usually a positive number).
 

Account Payments - The contract is paid but the payment never hits the register. The Transaction Edit report in the contract shows blank before and after statuses on the payment line. The Trans Not Match report will show if the contract is active, otherwise, change to Transhistory to see the historical view. You will need a Database Edit to pay the amount (the Miscellaneous Entry is usually a positive number).



Informational Steps

In this section, you will get more information about the Miscellaneous Entry by using the following steps.

Here's how to get there
Expert: Configuration > Accounting Configurations > Account Numbers
Elite: ⚙️ > App Configuration > Accounting Configurations > Account Numbers
 

1. Look at the customer’s call history/call logs. We have seen Miscellaneous Entries created as a result of a Database Edit that we did when attempting to correct another Miscellaneous Entry.

 

2. See if the customer has their Interstore Balance turned on. If they have a G/L Account for Interstore Balance, then no entry is made in the Error Log. Simply remove the account number for all stores and regenerate the G/L to see the Miscellaneous Entry. Another suggestion is to count all the Interstore Balances together, and if they do not equal, then that amount is the Miscellaneous Entry.

 

If the Interstore Balance has an account number, you will need to note what that value is and remove the account number. The reason we do this is so we can regenerate the G/L entry and get our Miscellaneous Entry total at the bottom (Miscellaneous Entries will always show under the last store).
 

1. Check the Error Log - Go into Database Management > Logs > Error Log.

           

Note: You will only do this step if the customer does not have their Interstore Balance turned on.
 

2. Regenerate the G/L Entry - Go into Accounting Reports > Accounting Reports Menu > Reprint G/L Entries > Select the date the Miscellaneous Entry happened.


Note: This step will only help if they have an Interstore Balance account. You can use this step if there is no correcting entry found. Otherwise, everything should be in the error log.


Sometimes reprinting the G/L will correct itself by removing the Miscellaneous Entry.

 

Note: If you do not know the date, a quick tip to see if they had a Miscellaneous Entry is to select the last day of the month and choose “Print G/L Entries from the 1st to the selected day”. This will give you the entire month’s G/L totals with a grand total of the Miscellaneous Entries. This will also tell you if there was a correcting entry for a Miscellaneous Entry. If you get a total for a Miscellaneous Entry, then work your way up the calendar week by week until you find the week/day that the entry happened.



Discovery Step

Generally, there are a few rules you can use to help identify where the Miscellaneous Entry might be located, depending on if the entry amount is negative or positive.

Negative Entry - The error is usually a contract that did not post and would be found in the Contract Posted to Totals.

Positive Entry - The error is more likely found in the Trans Not Match reports or could be a missing payment in the Payments NOT put into Cash Drawer.


In this section, you should know that the client has a Miscellaneous Entry. You should know the value and if it is a debit (negative entry) or credit (positive entry).
 

1. If the Miscellaneous Entry is negative (debit), then proceed with this step.
 

a. Run the TransNoMatchPosted report.
 

i. Go into Custom Reports > type in <PORDrive>:\POR\Reports and click View. Find and run TransNoMatchPosted report. Select the correct year.

ii. This report will contain all contracts that are dated within the selected year. The statuses of these contracts are Closed or Completed, where the posting does not match the contract value.
 

b. Run the ComparePostedTotals report.
 

i. Go into Custom Reports > type in <PORDrive>:\POR\Reports and click View. Find and run the ComparePostedTotals report. Enter the Batch number.

​​​​​​ii. Go through the pages and look for red numbers. This report will tell you if the Store Totals (TotalsCompare) match Contract Posted to Totals (TotalsContracts (Cash/Accrual)).

 
If your Miscellaneous Entry is in any one of these reports, note the contract number(s). To correct the entry, proceed to the Correction Steps for Contract Postings and go to the appropriate section.
 

1. If the Miscellaneous Entry is positive (credit), then proceed with this step.
 

Note: Generally, when a Miscellaneous Entry is negative, this usually means that a payment is missing.

  1.  

a. Run the TransNotMatchEdit report.

 

i.  Go into Custom Reports > type in <PORDrive>:\POR\Reports and click View. Find and run the TransNoMatchEdit report. Select the correct year.

ii.  This report will contain all contracts that are dated for the selected year.

 

b. Run Payment Report - Look for the value of the Miscellaneous Entry or items combined
that equals the total.

c. Run Operator Edit Report - Look for the value of the Miscellaneous Entry or items
combined that equals the total.

d. Run Disbursement Report - Look for the value of the Miscellaneous Entry or items
combined that equals the total.

e. Look at the G/L and see if there is an exact value that matches the Miscellaneous Entry.

 
If your Miscellaneous Entry is in any one of these reports, note the contract(s) number. To correct the entry, proceed to the Correction Steps for Missing Payment section.

Correction Steps for Missing Payment

This section assumes you have found the contract(s) that are causing the Miscellaneous Entry.


1. Inquire the contract in question.

2. View Transaction Edit.
 

a. Is the transaction missing a modification?   Yes  /  No


3. View Payment History.
 

a. Is the transaction missing a payment?    Yes  /  No


4. View the Contract.
 

a. Does the contract show paid?    Yes  /  No


If the contract is missing a modification and payment history but shows as paid, continue to the following steps:
 

Note: This is in Data Explorer > Transactions Table and should only be done if you are experienced in Database Edits.
 

Credit Cards - If their Credit Cards Totals balance, you will need to pay the contract with an ALT + B (Command + B for Mac users) bypass so it will not affect their credit card processor. If their Credit Cards Totals do not balance, you will let the client know to pay the contract by credit card, by either retrieving the card or manually entering the credit card information at the payment screen.

Cash - If the contract was paid by cash and the counter person took the cash from the customer, pay the contract by cash. If they did not take cash, do not pay the contract by cash.

Check - If the contract was paid by check and the counter person took a check from the customer, pay the contract by check. If they did not take a check, then do not pay the contract by check. If you are refunding the contract and the payment was taken by check, then make sure after refunding the money by check, that a disbursement is created to balance the cash drawer. If the contract is not showing paid but there is payment history and no Transaction Edit, continue to the following:

 

1. Get Contract Paid Total: __________

 

2. Run Database Edit.

 

Note: This Database Edit should only be done if you are experienced in Database Edits.
