Business Area 


Introduction

Invoices generated for different depots in Syrinx are generally transferred to a single accounts system company. If there are two separate parts of the company or two different account companies, then generally in order to produce separate sets of invoices for each accounts company, you would have two separate Syrinx databases, one for each accounts system company. However, this means that stock, customers etc. in Syrinx cannot be shared between the two Syrinx databases as they are separate entities.

If the requirement is to maintain a single set of stock and customers within a single Syrinx database but to still be able to split the transfer of invoices from one or more depots into separate accounts system companies then “Business Areas” will provide this functionality.

A business area can be thought of as a single accounts system company. Within Syrinx, a business area can be created and linked to one or more depots, where invoices generated for those depots will be transferred to the accounts system company that is associated with the business area.

Configuration

In the following example we will create two business areas; one for depots in England, and one for depots in Scotland. We have two accounts system companies in our accounts package, one for England the other for Scotland.

A patch will need to be created by POR implementation or support to add business areas to Syrinx as these cannot be added via the user interface. In order to create the patch, the following information will be required:

  • Business Area Name (Mandatory)
  • Name to Appear on Invoices (Optional)
  • Invoice Address (Optional)
  • Vat Number (Mandatory)

Also the default behaviour for transferring customers from Syrinx to your accounts system is that customers will be transferred to all business areas. It is possible to alter this behaviour via a patch so that customers will only be transferred to the business area associated with the main depot that is set on the customer's account. If the later behaviour is required, please make sure POR implementation or support are aware of this so that the patch can be modified.

In order to transfer to the two separate accounts companies, two Syrinx Transfer Tool configurations will need to be created in the Syrinx Transfer tool used to export invoice information to your accounts package, one for each business area. In the Syrinx Data tab of the transfer tool configuration settings screen, a new "Bus Area:" field is shown which should have the name of the business area entered.
