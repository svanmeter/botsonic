INTERNAL | End of Day



 

Understanding the End of Day process is essential as it can immediately impact our customer’s ability to do business. End of Day support requests need immediate attention and resolution! Understanding the End of Day process is important as troubleshooting mistakes can lead to totals and system errors. 

 

All employees should familiarize themselves with the End of Day processes  to ensure you’re taking the correct steps to address End of Day support requests.

What is End of Day?

 

When a company performs an End of Day, they close their books for a business day or period. We recommend that customers close daily, however there are customers who close only a few times a month. It’s also important to know that the End of Day can be configured to run automatically. 


What is the End of Day Process?

 

There are a couple of ways the End of Day can be run. Follow the below links to learn more about each method.

 
  • Manually -  This is the standard process for End of Day (Close Drawer for End of Day).
  • Autoclose - This method allows our customers to close automatically either by system initiation or user initiation (Auto Close Drawer).

What do Credit Cards have to do with End of Day?

 

Point of Rental records cash, checks, and credit card totals, which means that credit cards are also a part of the End of Day process. Customers can run the Check & Credit Card Report to compare the totals recorded in POR and their credit card provider. Our customers can integrate their credit cards with POR but it is not required.

 

If the customer is integrated, Point of Rental will ensure the computed totals (the amount recorded in POR) matches what the customer has physically taken with their credit card device (also known as a Gateway). If the amounts do not balance, the customer  WILL NOT be able to continue the End of Day. For more information, see our article End of Day Imbalance Troubleshooting.

 

If the customer is NOT integrated, the credit card total can be counted, adjusted, and confirmed during the closing process within the Cash Drawer Balancing window (the same window where users count their petty cash).
