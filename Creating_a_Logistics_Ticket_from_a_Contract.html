Creating a Logistics Ticket from a Contract




This article will discuss creating a logistic ticket from a contract. L-tickets can also be created from within the Dispatch Center. This feature allows you to break out items from contracts to logistics tickets that can then be assigned to as many trucks as needed at different times. Or if your customer wants to will-call part of the order you can break the items up to handle the scenario operationally. This feature is best used with the Dispatch Center.

Important!  Logistics Tickets DO NOT affect inventory availability. Item availability is still controlled by the parent contract. Also, if you're tracking weight for dispatch center reporting, you will need to move all items on the contract to a logistics ticket. This is to avoid the contract showing overweight.

Creating a Logistics Ticket from a contract


Once the Logistics Ticket is saved, your dispatcher will see both contracts in his daily routes, allowing him to schedule the pickup or delivery of the equipment. The line items on the logistics ticket will be suppressed on the parent contract. 
 

1. Once the original contract has been created or modified, you can select Logistics Tickets from the Menu Screen. To get to this menu, you must save the contract and then follow the prompts until you arrive to this window. 
 



 

2. The screen will split, showing the items on the contract on the left. Select the Out or In radio button to designate the Logistics ticket as a delivery or pickup. If the delivery or pickup box is not checked on the L-ticket then no contract will show in the dispatch center. 
 

  • Selecting the Out option will show the new L-ticket as a delivery.
  • Selecting the In option will show the L-ticket as a pickup within Dispatch center. 





3. Move the items you would like to put on the L-Ticket or logistics ticket by checking the box next to the line items highlighting the items you want to move and using the [>>>] arrows. The items will still appear on the original ticket, for billing purposes.
 

  • You cannot move over header records to a logistics ticket.
  • A copy of the items will be entered onto the logistic ticket.
 




4. You will be prompted with the Edit Contract Info box to enter the name of the logistics ticket. We recommend using the customer or event name.

 

5. The new name of the L-Ticket will be shown in the upper right corner of the ticket. The ticket will be assigned a Logistic Number and updated status. Also, the delivery date and time from the original contract will carry over to the L-ticket. 


6. You can add any comments or Delivery/Pickup Notes by selecting their respective tabs.


7. Once all the items have been moved to the L-ticket, click on the Save button at the bottom right of the window.

8. Once saved, the dispatcher will see both contracts in his daily routes, allowing him to schedule the pickup or delivery of the equipment.
 

 

 



Where can I see the Logistics Tickets I created?

You can do this in two places:

  1.  Within the contract, under Reports > Related contracts.
  2.  You'll also be able to see the logistics ticket within the Dispatch Center on the date of the delivery/pickup.

Transferring items with Logistics Tickets
 

Logistics Tickets created off of repair contracts are used to transfer broken items between stores without overbooking on a transfer contract. The item, item’s repair contract, and any un-pulled sales items on the contract will be transferred to the store that closes that logistics ticket.
