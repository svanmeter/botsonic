INTERNAL | End of Day Common Error Guide

 

 

Below are the most common issues customers encounter during the End of Day process. This document is in relation to users not being able to open contracts due to the End of Day not finishing correctly.




Topics in this article include
 

  • Multi-store not being able to close (General EOD Troubleshooting)
  • Error! You are already closing for the day on another terminal.
  • Warning! You must wait until End of Day is finished...
  • Waiting for Store #__ to finish Closing
  • Waiting for Store # __ to finish Closing BUT all stores have closed
  • Cannot modify contracts for Multi-store set to auto-close
  • Clearing an End of Day Lock
  • Non-Matching PID Number in Task Manager and Locks Table
  • The Customer wants to close for the day prior on the End of Month
  • The Customer wants to close their drawer for the day prior AFTER closing for EOM/Y




Multi-store not being able to close (General EOD Troubleshooting)


For multi-store customers, stores not closing can be a common issue that arises at End of the Day. The below image is a sample from the End of Day History report. There are a couple of key pieces of information in this report that can be used when the customer is experiencing an issue with stores not closing or users being unable to access the Counter System or access/create new contracts.

 

Here’s how to get there

Expert - 2. End of Day Processing > End of Day History Report

Elite - Right Menu > Reports Search

 

 

a. Here are the best ways to confirm if all stores have closed:

 

i. By looking at the End of Day History report > Store column.

At the top of the report, stores that have closed will be listed in sequential order (see above image). If a store or stores are not displaying, the missing store(s) needs to run or complete theirs by the end of the day.


ii. By looking at the End of Day History report > Batch and Active Batch number.

You can confirm this information by looking at the batch number located next to the store and the active batch displayed above the listed stores (see highlighted columns in the image above).

 

1. The active batch cannot be the same as the batch number of the store number.

 

a. If it is, then your next step is to look at the locks table from within the report. To do this press the I icon.

 

 

b. A list of locks will display. You may see a 1400 numbered lock (DO NOT CLEAR LOCKS). In most cases, the computer from store 1 will need to log in, to finish by the end of day.

 

 

 

iii. By looking at the Locks Table.

To get there from within Expert, go to #10 Security > Database Locks.

From Elite, go to the Gear icon> Security> Database Locks. You’ll be able to see the same information above in the report. However, it does display more information. DO NOT CLEAR LOCKS. Clearing End of Day locks will cause miscellaneous entries with the accounting general ledger file. This can’t be corrected.

 

b. It is also important to know the differences between manual and automatic closings. The End of Day report will display whether the closing was automatically or manually closed.

 

 

i. Automatic Closings - the Point of Rental system will close the store instead of a user. You can identify if the store was closed automatically by looking at the End of Day report and looking at the Employee column. This column will show the user who ran the End of the Day. An autoclosed store will display “Autoclose '' in red under the Employee column.
 

1. Auto-close can be initiated in 2 ways:

 

a. By the Point of Rental system. Within the report under the Employee column, if there is a “ * ” before the word “Autoclose'', it means the Autoclose option has been enabled within the Parameters > Drawer tab of that store. Keep in mind that this box will only autoclose remotes stores. If store 1 needs to be set to autoclose then a task in task runner needs to be setup.

b. By a User. If Autoclose appears without the “ * ”, it means a user pressed the Autoclose button found in #2 End of Day Processing end of day or Store 1 has a Taskrunner set to autoclose.

 

 

ii. Manual Closings - a user initiates the End of the Day by pressing the Close Drawer for End of the Day button.

 



Error! You are already closing for the day on another terminal.
 

If your customer receives the below error message, it means a user at their store location pressed the button “Close Drawer for End of Day”, initiating the End of Day process. The instance of End of Day is running.

 

 

Resolution:

 

i. Find the computer where the End of Day instance is running.

 

1. You can do this by going to the Locks Table or by pressing the i-icon at the End of Day report (as shown in the above section). Find the End of the Day lock, and identify the computer or terminal that initiated the End of Day process.

 

a. If you go to the Locks Table, DO NOT CLEAR ANY LOCKS. DO NOT LET THE CUSTOMERS CLEAR LOCKS. Clearing End of Day locks will cause miscellaneous entries with the accounting general ledger file. This can’t be corrected.

 

ii. Complete the End of Day process on the computer that initiated the End of Day.

 

1. You can do this by pressing Close Drawer for the End of Day if the EoD processing screen is not already open.

 



Warning! You must wait until End of Day is finished...


If your customer is receiving the message below, they are attempting to open the Counter System in Expert or write a contract in Elite. The message is notifying the customer that their End of Day process is currently running in their store or other stores (if multi-store setup) and that they must finish their End of Day.

 

 

Resolution:

 

i. Run the End of Day History report.

 

1. If multi-store, identify which store(s) need to complete their end of day. For further detail on how to do this, see Section 1 in this document.

 

2. If a single store, press the i-icon to see which computer initiated the end of day and complete it on that terminal.

 

ii. Let the customer know to complete their End of Day.



 

Waiting for Store #__ to finish Closing
 

If your customer is receiving the message below, it means the customer is a multi-store company. This message is received when a couple or several stores are waiting for other stores to complete their End the Day process. For multi-store companies, all stores need to run their End of the Day, before any other store can continue to create/modify contracts.

 

 

Resolution:

 

i. Have the customer contact the store location displayed in the pop-up message and have them complete their End of the Day. The message will disappear once all stores have completed their End of Day.

 

ii. If Cancel is selected, then a user with Regional Manager’s password level or higher will be prompted to enter credentials. This in turn will ‘autoclose’ that store. This can be done for each store except Store 



 

Waiting for Store # __ to finish Closing BUT all stores have closed
 

 If your customer is receiving the message below, it means the customer is a multi-store company. This message is received when a couple or several stores are waiting for other stores to complete their End the Day process. However, the End of Day instance can become stuck even if all stores have completed their closing.

 

 

Resolution:

 

i. Confirm that the End of the Day instance is stuck.

 

1. Open the Locks Table. Below is an example of a multi-store company locks table. DO NOT CLEAR ANY LOCKS. DO NOT LET THE CUSTOMERS CLEAR LOCKS. Clearing End of Day locks will cause miscellaneous entries with the accounting general ledger file. This can’t be corrected.

 

 

 

a. Notice in the first column that the file name is “End of Day”. These are instances of the End of Day.

·  In the Number column, it is displaying the End of Day instance for each store. These will display as inactive.
·  1= Store 1.
·  There will also be a 1400 lock.

b. Notice the 1400 lock of 1407 and the End of Day instance for store 1 is inactive.

 

ii. Confirm that all stores have completed their close.

 

iii. In this scenario, the customer will have to log into the computer that initiated the end of day from store 1 and finish the End of Day. 

 

iv. You will receive a prompt asking if you want to finish the close. Select yes and the system will finish the end-of-day process.



 

Cannot modify contracts for Multi-store set to auto-close
 

In this scenario, a multi-store company is set to auto-close but no one can modify transactions or access the Counter System. The below image is the locks table for this scenario. 

 

 

Resolution:

 

i. Confirm that the customer is using auto-close.

1. You can do this by going to the locks table and looking for the “STR1Admin” user under the User column. DO NOT CLEAR ANY LOCKS. DO NOT LET THE CUSTOMERS CLEAR LOCKS.

Clearing End of Day locks will cause miscellaneous entries with the accounting general ledger file. This cannot be corrected.

a. This means that Store 1 is set to auto-close but somehow end of day is stuck somewhere in the close.

b. If you cannot find this user, the customer may not have auto-close enabled in Parameters and may have instead manually auto-closed. To confirm, run the End of the Day History report.

 

ii. Confirm that all stores have completed their end of day.

1. You can do this by running the End of the Day History report.

2. If they have not, have all stores finish closing.

 

iii. Confirm that the 1407 lock is “Running” within the locks table.

1. You can do this by going back to the locks table and seeing the running status on the same row as the 1407 lock

a. This means that the ARACD1 task is “running” on the server but is stuck.

 

2. If not, open a store 1 terminal and run the End of the Day.

 

iv. Open the task manager > Details tab.

1. You can do this by right-clicking the taskbar on the server and selecting Task Manager.

 

 

 2. Once the Task Manager appears, go to the Details tab.

 

 

v. On the server, end the task named “ARACD1.”

1. To do this, find the task named “ARACD1,” right-click, and select End Task.

 

 

2. Within the Locks Table, this will change the Store 1 end of day instance and 1407 lock to “Not Running.”

 

vi. Run the End of Day again from any Store 1 computer.

1. You will be prompted to finish the close, select yes. This will pick up the end of the day where the close left off.

a. By ending the task in the task manager you will bypass the lock while avoiding the loss of information.

 

 

Clearing an End of Day Lock

 

In this section, you’ll learn about clearing an End of Day lock. There is only one exemption for when an End of Day lock can be cleared; in the below scenario, no serious effects occur as a result of clearing an End of Day lock.

 

 

  1. Identify the “Not Running” End of Day lock and confirm the lock is a 100 lock.
  2. Write down the Job PID number.
a. You can do this by going to the locks table and looking at the Running column.
  1. Confirm in the Task Manager that the End of Day lock is “Not Running.”
a. You can do this by opening the Task Manager on the same server where the customer’s Point of Rental is located.
 
i. To open the Task Manager through Teamviewer, right-click on the customer's Taskbar.
ii. If they cannot, have the customer’s IT department give you a connection where you can access the Task Manager.
b. Go to the Details tab
c. Sort the PID column number and look for the Job PID number you wrote down earlier.
i. IF THE PID NUMBER DOES NOT MATCH PLEASE SEE THE NEXT SECTION "Non-Matching PID number in task manager and locks table."
d. Confirm the Task is not responsive.
 
   4. Confirm that there is no entry for the attempted End of Day at the End of Day History report.
   5. Go back to the Locks table and clear the lock by double-clicking the lock.
 
DO NOT PRESS CLEAR. THIS WILL CLEAR ALL LOCKS.
 

 

Non-matching PID Number in Task Manager and Locks Table

 

This is a process identification used by Windows that can be verified on the server as an administrator user. This is done in the Task Manager> Details tab (Note: the tab may be different depending on the year of the server or the Windows version).

 

You should be able to match the JOB PID number and the PID number in the Task Manager to confirm if the lock is truly inactive or active. The numbers should match.

 

There is a rare occurrence when another program or task is using the same PID as the End of Day Job PID. This can cause issues trying to close. The system will see the lock as running even though ARACD1 is not running on the server. It is not known how this happens. Clearing the program from the Task Manager may disrupt the windows program as well.

 

Resolution:

 

  1. Get all users off the server.
  2. Reboot the server.
    1. This should automatically clear that PID number.
  3. You should be able to finish the End of Day close by using the above Troubleshooting Steps.

 

 

 

The Customer wants to Close for the Day Prior on End of Month

 

In this scenario, a customer wants to close their drawer for the day prior. However, the current day is the end of the month.


Resolution

i. Initiate the End of Day using the “Close for Specific Day” option found within the Support Panel.

1. You can do this by opening the support panel.

a. To do this in Expert: open the Program Menu and double-click on the date and enter the POR Admin Date Code.

 

 

 

b. To do this in Elite:

i. Right-click on the upper left-hand corner of the application.

 

 

 

ii. This will launch POR File Sync in Admin Mode. Go to Tools > Support Panel or press this key combination (Ctrl + S).

 

 

 

 

2. Within the Support Panel, select Tools, in the top-menu bar, and then select the option “Close for a Specific Day.”

 

 

 

 

3. A warning message will appear, shown in the image below. Select Yes.

 

 

4. Enter the date specified by the customer and press OK. In this scenario, the customer is attempting to close for the day before the End of Month. The system will ask you to confirm the date again.

 

 

5. The system will then give you another prompt to confirm the date entered. If correct, proceed and select yes.

 

 

6. Multistore ONLY: If the customer is a multistore, a prompt will appear allowing you to select which store to close. When selected, press “Launch POR.”

 

 

7. Follow the EOD process from here.

 

ii. If the above scenario does not result in a solution, this may need to require the server time be set to the night prior. This option will require all users to be out of the program. This will need to be brought to IT’s attention. This is considered a SYSTEM DOWN.

 


The customer wants to close their drawer for the day prior AFTER closing for EOM/Y

In this scenario, a customer wants to close their drawer for the day prior. However, the customer has already closed for the End of Month/Year. 

 

Resolution:

This is not possible. They will not be able to close again until the next business day.

1. To see if the customer has closed for End of Month/Year, run the End of Day history report and look for the EOM column. Y = Yes. N = No.

 

 

a. During the End of Month and End of Year, Point of Rental does several things in the background that you might not be able to see.

 

i. It recomputes depreciation and will determine the Item Income.

ii. It also updates several tables like the Customer File, Item File, and Vendor File to name a few. It is vital that you DO NOT CLEAR LOCKS during this time.

iii. If the customer is integrated with Credit Cards, during the year end process the system will unencrypt and re-encrypt credit card tokens that are stored within the Point of Rental. So, new tokens are created for those cards.
