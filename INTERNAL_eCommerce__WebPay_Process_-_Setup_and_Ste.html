Step 1: Initial Steps


For our WebPay module setup, we need four pieces of information related to the Point of Rental TSYS account: Merchant #, Site ID, Merchant Key, and Web API Key. Some of this information may be found in the POR database. The following steps will make this as easy as possible and speed up the implementation process.

*POR version 2022 and latest patches/updates required*
 

Please utilize the space below to store this required information:

Merchant #:

Site ID:

Merchant Key:

Web API Key:

 

1. Merchant #:

Go to POR Configuration --> System
Configurations --> Parameters and select the
“Drawer” tab. Then copy &
paste the value from the field “Merchant #” into
the designated area above next to “Merchant #.”
 

Note: The Merchant # in POR is an alphabetic value. We can't use your TSYS
merchant number.

 

2. Site ID & Merchant Key:

Go to POR Security --> Verifone Terminals --> Credit Card Terminals and select Store #1. Then copy & paste the values from the fields “Site Id” and “Merchant Key” into the designated areas above.

 

3. Obtaining the Web API Key from Cayan/TSYS:
 

  1. Contact your direct Cayan/TSYS Sales Rep or Cayan/TSYS Sales/Support via phone or email.
  2. Give them the Merchant #, Site Id and Merchant Key which you gathered from your POR and ask them to email you your “Web API Key.”

Along with the four pieces of information related to the Point of Rental Cayan/TSYS account: Merchant #, Site ID, Merchant Key, and Web API Key, we also need answers to a few basic questions which clients can answer below:
 

  1. Will you be sending out the WebPay link with Quotes as well as Reservations?

  2. What email address do you wish to receive your payment notifications? Please choose only one.

  3. Do you wish to: (choose ONLY 1 - cannot combine multiple options)

    • allow the customer to pay a fixed percentage of the reservation’s total? This would be a fixed
      percentage for every reservation i.e. 50%. An option will also be displayed to pay 100% if the
      customer wishes. Note: This is the case with most Party/Event Stores.

OR

  • only collect 100% of the amount due on every reservation?

OR

  • collect only a deposit which you enter in Counter System when you create & email the reservation
    to the customer?

OR

  • collect the total amount of the reservation AND a deposit which you enter in Counter System when you create the reservation?

 

After reviewing the above 3 items, please have the client answer accordingly.

If there are any other details regarding the payments that they’d like us to know, please have them type them it in the file/doc as well.

Now it's time to provide this information to the web team! Once they receive the email message from Cayan/TSYS containing your “WEB API KEY”, have them forward the email to support@rentalhosting.com. Also have them forward a copy of their temporary file/document containing your POR Merchant #, Site Id, Merchant Key, and setup information to support@rentalhosting.com.

Once all four pieces of information are collected, have them initial at the bottom of the official "Initial WebPay Instructions" document, which is linked above.

Now let the client know that their WebPay module is in the works, and to stay tuned for next steps!

Once we have these credentials (Rental Hosting team) we will implement them on our end on the server internally. The next steps will be sent after we complete our initial implementation.



Step 2: Creating Email Template(s) & Sending a Test Reservation
 

Now that the Web Team has started the implementation of the WebPay module, there are a few small tasks for the client to do:

 

Create one or more email templates

This email will replace the current email that you send to customers with their quote and/or reservation/contract. If you plan to accept online payments for quotes AND reservations/contracts, then you will most likely need to create a new email template for each.
 

1. In POR NetHelp, search for “web payment”.

2. Open the NetHelp page titled “Web Payment Configuration/Setup”.

3. Follow the instructions to create a new email template for quotes and for reservations to
include the WebPayment Link. They may want to copy the body of the email from
their current template to the new one, then add text about the online payment, along with the Special Field.

4. For now (until we complete our testing), make sure that they do not set this email template as the default template to use by their staff.
 

Create a test quote and/or reservation
 

1. Create a test quote and/or reservation from their POR Counter System and change the reservation value to $1 before emailing it to support@rentalhosting.com

2. Email it to support@rentalhosting.com using their new POR email template + WebPayment link.

Now let the client know that their WebPay module is being tested, and to stay tuned for next steps!

Once we have the test web payment link (Rental Hosting team) we will make the test payment, and make sure all is good on our end. The final steps will be sent after we complete our testing.



Step 3: Confirm Deposited Payment & Process Refund from Counter System


All that's left to do is confirm our test payment, and then to process a refund on that test payment! I believe POR Support normally helps clients with this. Please:
 

1. Confirm that the $1.00 payment was imported/posted in their POR for the specific test contract.

2. Process a refund for $1.00 to confirm that the CC token passed to their POR is valid for refunds.


If clients ever need assistance with this, we direct them to POR support for help as we do not have knowledge of the refund process.

Once the above 3 steps are completed, the clients' WebPay module will be ready to use with real customers' reservations, and we are good to go!

 

Desired Deposits
 

If a client selects to use the Desired Deposit feature for a set dollar amount to be entered from their POR, Rental Hosting must NOT use the percentage amount deposit entered on the RH end, and instead instruct the customer to use the Desired Deposit feature within their POR. This can ONLY be entered on a Quote Level contract, in the "Desired Deposit" box of the Quote. RH also must enable a flag on their end to get this Desired Deposit to show up on the Web Payment page (CCPaymentPageShowDepositOnly for our internal use), which will enable ONLY the desired deposit to show up on the Web payment page. If the customer does not enter a desired deposit on the quote, it will default to show a 100% payment option.
