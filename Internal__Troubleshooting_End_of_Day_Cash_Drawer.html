INTERNAL | End of Day Cash Drawer Troubleshooting

 


 

This article will cover the basics of troubleshooting any end of day calls.

The following screenshot shows the End of Day Functions menu which can be found by selecting End of Day Processing from the Program Menu. This menu is where the customer will find various reports helpful in troubleshooting end of day problems.




Topics in this article include:

 

  • Cash

  • Credit Cards (Not Integrated)

  • Credit Cards (Integrated)

  • Steps to Troubleshoot

  • Locks

 

 

 

 

Cash

If cash totals are off when the customer calls, here are the steps to take for troubleshooting. Make sure to know how much is off based on what the customer says.


Note: When it comes to cash, customer care can only go off what Point of Rental Software reports.
 

  1. Review End of Day History Report (Totals Summary) to see what the carryover cash was set to on the last close.

    1. Make sure checks that were taken on the last close were deposited.

      1. If checks were not deposited, then they will roll over as carryover cash the next day.  

      2. If that is the amount that is off, then include that amount as the cash count. 

    2. If the amount is not the checks, then proceed to the next step. 

  2. After reviewing carryover cash, make note of that amount and run the Operator Edit Report for the current date. This report will display all the cash that was taken for that date selected. 

    1. Verify with the customer that the totals are correct. 

    2. Have the customer recount cash. 

  3. If the above steps do not reflect the difference in their cash, the customer will have to address that. They will need to make sure all transactions are accounted for and make sure payments were made with the correct payment type. 


 

Credit Cards (Not Integrated)


This section outlines what to do if a customer calls stating their cards do not match. Since the customer is not integrated there is not much to do except for the following.
 

  1. Run the Check & Card Report for the current day.

  2. Have the customer compare the total of the report to their credit card machine statement.

 
 

Credit Cards (Integrated)

A customer calls stating their cards do not match. Being credit card integrated, the first thing customers can do is verify that the cards are off via Credit Card Inquiry. From the Credit Card Inquiry screen, it is important to know which gateway the customer is using. Also, run the Check & Card Report for the current day to compare processed transactions.

The option will say in Credit Card Inquire either Moneris (Canada), Point, or Cayan. If the screen says Nova, then the customer is on PC Charge.


Note: If using PC Charge, since it is not supported, this will be a charge of $75 an hour. This file will be located in C:\Program Files (x86)\Active Charge\ and look for a file type of .enc. Please make a copy of this file before you open it. If you have questions reach out to a Tier 2.


If the customer is using Moneris, then they will need to log into their merchant account and compare transactions to the Check & Card Report.

 

Rule of Thumb

It is important when running Credit Card Inquire to know whether or not the processor is higher or lower than Point of Rental’s totals after selecting the submit button. Point of Rental’s totals can be seen from the Check & Card Report.


 

Steps to Troubleshoot

If the processor is higher, most likely there is a charge that the processor has that Point of Rental did not record. If the processor is lower, then either an amount was charged after the end of day on the prior day, or there is a duplicate refund. If the customer is on Point, then most likely Point of Rental settled the first transaction of the day.
 

1. If the customer is on Cayan or Point, from the Credit Card Inquire press alt + L on the keyboard. This will expand a window to show all transactions for the current day.


Note: Do NOT advise the customer of this function.
 

2. Next, compare the charges to the Check & Card Report.

a. If a charge or a refund is duplicated (same charge or refund twice on the same card), then double click the charge or refund to void.
 

Note: Voids can only be done on the same day as the charge and may take several minutes to update. Otherwise, the customer will need to log in to their merchant account to refund. The processor always settles transactions at midnight.


b. If a contract is not accurately reflecting a payment, make a standard payment by credit card on the contract. Once you get to the credit card processing screen, press alt + b. This will bypass the credit card processor. Then enter the POR Admin day code.
 


 


 

c. If the customer is on Point and the first transaction is settled, go to Credit Card Inquire and then force settle the batch by left double-clicking Batch Type. Enter the POR Admin password and then select submit.
 


The Response and Status fields will display “Settlement Schedule” and “Success.”

d. Click cancel and proceed to the end of the day processing where the customer will be prompted to enter the password twice.
 


 

e. If a payment was taken on a previous day, then proceed with the same steps as above to settle the batch.


 

Locks

Never clear a 14xx lock or a running lock. This will cause issues with the General Ledger in Point of Rental that cannot be reversed.

If you see the end of day locks for a store and they have 1401 – 1415 record numbers, simply running end of the day again will cause the program to pick-up where it left off and finish without creating a second totals summary entry and removing the necessity to run finish close.

1407 – Waiting for a remote store to close. You will only see this in store 1.

If you see 1408 or 2408, they killed off the aracd1.exe program. You can rerun the end of the day, and Point of Rental will know where to complete, if there is a 14xx lock.

There is only one time a lock can be cleared. You MUST follow these criteria below:

This means someone started End of Day but did not finish.
 

  • There must not be an entry in the End of Day History (Total Summary) for that store.
     

  • Lock will be JOB PID 100.
 

Note: This number can be different.

 

  • Verified that it is not running in the locks table.
     

  • Additional: you do not want to see a aracd1.exe running in the task manager in Windows.
