 When do I transfer a case to the CC team?
 

  • Un-integrating
  • Pricing Review
  • Closing a Credit Card Account
  • Bank Account and Ownership changes
     
 

 I need to transfer a case to the Credit Card team

If you catch one of these phone calls (listed above), all you need to do is create a case (separate from the case that you are working), add Credit Card Account Review in the subject line and place it in the CC Implementation Queue. The credit card team will take over.

 Un-integrating Credit Card Requests

As we all know, there is a lot to know and retain about credit cards from how to handle certain situations and who else to get involved, to who is responsible for the issue at hand, and if it is billable.

The last thing we want to do is add anything else to the list. However, I am afraid we must.
 

If we have a customer call in and they are wanting to do something such as un-integrate their credit cards from their Point of Rental system – for any reason – this is not a simple request. Sure, you can go into Parameters and disable the integration but that part is simple. However, there are other factors we must take into consideration.

 

Does the customer use POINT or CAYAN?
 
  • If the customer is on POINT are we cancelling their POINT account? If yes, this is handled by the Point of Rental credit card team.
 
  • If they are on POINT, was their device Leased or Purchased? If leased, the devices will need to be returned to VeriFone. There is documentation that has to be completed by the Point of Rental credit card team.
 
  • If the customer is on Cayan, the customer must go to Cayan to get the account shut down and to handle any hardware they have received from Cayan.
 

Who is the customer's processor? If their processor is Elavon, did we set them up with Elavon?
 

  • If Point of Rental set them up with Elavon, then Point of Rental will need to submit the necessary forms to get the account closed and to remove all monthly/annual rates and fees. This would be handled by our credit card team.
 
  • If they use a 3rd party processor, then the customer is responsible for getting any unused merchant accounts shut down.
