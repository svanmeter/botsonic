INTERNAL | End of Month/End of Year Common Issues

 

 

 

This article discusses common issues for End of Month and End of Year in Point of Rental. During the End of Month and End of Year, Point of Rental does several things in the background that you might not be able to see. During the End of Month, one of the big things is that the software re-computes depreciation and how item income is determined. During the End of the Year, the software not only re-computes depreciation but also updates several records like the Customer File, Item File, and Vendor File to name a few. It is vital that you do not clear any locks during this time.

 

Topics included in this article

 

  • What happens at End of Day/Month/Year?
  • Reading the EOD Report
  • Close for a Day Prior
  • Stores that Close for EOM/EOY

 



What happens at End of Day/Month/Year?

If the customer is integrated with credit cards, the year-end process unencrypts and re-encrypts the credit card tokens that are in the Point of Rental system. This means that POR creates new tokens for credit cards in the system. It also prints the EOY Customer dollars to report. This is determined by the parameter setting in the Reports tab, displayed below. The customer should make sure that they have a dollar amount specified in the box.

Once the EOD starts printing, there's no way to stop this process.

 


 

Reading the EOD Report

For multi-store customers, stores not closing can be a common issue that arises for the End of Day process. The below image is a sample from the End of Day History Report. There are a couple of key pieces of information in this report that can be used when the customer is experiencing an issue with stores not closing, users being unable to access the Counter System, or accessing/creating new contracts.

Here’s how to get there
Expert: Program Menu > End of Day Processing > End of Day History Report
Elite: Right Menu > End of Day History Report

 


Here are the best ways to confirm if all stores have closed:

 

i. By looking at the End of Day History Report > Store column. At the top of the report, stores that have closed will be listed in sequential order (see the above image). If a store or stores are not displaying, the missing store(s) needs to run or complete their end of day.

 

ii. By looking at the End of Day History Report > Batch and Active Batch number. You can confirm this information by looking at the batch number located next to the store and the active batch displayed above the listed stores (see highlighted columns in the image above).

 

1. The active batch cannot be the same as the batch number of the store number.

 

a. If it is, then your next step is to look at the locks table from within the report. To do this press the I icon.

 

 

b. A list of locks will display. You may see a 1400 numbered lock (DO NOT CLEAR LOCKS). In most cases, the computer from Store 1 will need to log in to finish the end of day process.

 

 

iii. By looking at the Locks Table. To get there from within Expert, go to Security > Database Locks. From Elite, go to Configuration Gear > Security > Database Locks. You’ll be able to see the same information above in the report. However, it does display more information. DO NOT CLEAR LOCKS. Clearing End of Day locks will cause miscellaneous entries with the accounting general ledger file. This can’t be corrected.

 

1. It is also important to know the differences between manual and automatic closings. The End of Day report will display whether the closing was automatically or manually closed.

 

 

iv. Automatic Closings - the Point of Rental system will close the store instead of a user. You can identify if the store was closed automatically by looking at the End of Day report and looking at the Employee column. This column will show the user who ran the End of the Day. An auto-closed store will display “Autoclose '' in red under the Employee column.

 

Autoclose can be initiated in 2 ways:

 

a. By the Point of Rental system. Within the report under the employee column, if there is an “ * ” before the word "Autoclose", it means the Autoclose option has been enabled within the Parameters > Drawer tab setting of that store.

 

b. By a User. If Autoclose appears without the “ * ”, it means a user pressed the Autoclose button found in End of Day Processing or Store 1 has a Taskrunner set to Autoclose.

 

ii. Manual Closings - A user initiates the End of the Day process by pressing the Close Drawer for End of the Day button.
 

 

a. When working with Month End you want to make sure that the EOM shows yes. If not, then you should look at the EOM in Calendar Maintenance.

 

 

 

Close for a Day Prior

During the last day of the month, if a store forgets to close their drawer for the day before, then they must use the ‘Close for Day’ tool in the Support Panel to close for the prior day. If this option does not work, then you will have to set back the server time to the night prior. This option will require all users to be out of the program.

Expert

Here's how to get there
Expert: Program Menu > double-click on the date > type in the POR Admin Date Code

 

 

Elite

Here's how to get there
Elite: Right-click on the upper left-hand corner of the application.

 

 

This will launch POR File Sync in Admin Mode.
 

1. Next, you will need to go to Tools > Support Panel (Ctrl + S). The Support Panel will give you the ability to perform other functions as well.

 

 

2. Go to Tools > Close for Specific Day.

 

 

3. This will bring up a warning message “Do you want to initiate Close For Day for a Specific Date?" Click Yes.
 

Note: You will select the store next if multi-store.

 

 

4. Then enter the date that you want to close.

5. Click OK. Then it will ask you to confirm the date again.

 

 

Then it will ask if you are sure you want to close for that date. If you are multistore, then it will have you choose the store that you are wanting to close.

 

 

6. Choose the store and click Launch POR.

 

 

7. Follow the EOD process from here.

 

 

Stores that Close for EOM/EOY


If a store has closed already for the End of Month or the End of Year, then we will not be able to close again till the next business day. You can see if they closed out the End of Month by going to the End of Day History Report.
