Overview

If you hire or sell to a large customer that has many contacts that could place orders, across many sites, you may want to enforce some controls so that only particular people can place orders.  To handle this, you can flag the customer as requiring formal contact identification.  

To use this feature, you must give one of your staff responsibility for:

  • identifying which of your customers require contact identification, and for each such customer:
  • allocating id numbers and pass codes for those customer contacts that are allowed to place orders
  • informing each contact of their unique number and pass code

Once set up, before any new hire contract or advice note can be raised (for any of these customers) your hire desk staff will be required to enter the contact’s unique identification number, plus the contact’s pass code (both of which must be provided by the person placing the order).

Amend Customers – Administering the allocation of Identification numbers

Your chosen administrator will have an additional ‘Contact Ids’ tab on the ‘Amend Customers’ screen:

To indicate that a particular customer requires contact identification, query the customer, and then tick the ‘Requires Contact Identification?’ box.

The ‘Managed By’ field can be used to pick another Syrinx user that is to be allowed to administer the contact identification for the selected customer.  This allows your overall administrator to delegate responsibility for certain customers to other Syrinx users.   When a non-administrator user (i.e. a user without the ‘Manage Contact Identification’ system privilege) opens the Amend Customers screen they will only have this tab on those customers that they have been flagged as a manager of.

The ‘Enable?’ box should be ticked for those customer contacts that are allowed to place orders for new hires / sales.  The Identification number and Pass code are generated automatically (if so configured in the System Options screen).  Generated Identification numbers are randomly generated 5 digit numbers. Generated pass codes are a letter followed by two digits, again randomly generated.  If the system is not auto-generating them, then they must be entered by the user.  

The ‘On Hold?’ box may be ticked to temporarily suspend a particular contact from placing new orders.

The Print button produces a report of all the contacts that have been enabled, along with their numbers and pass codes.  This report can be run for all customers or just the selected customer, and for all contacts, or just those enabled that day.

The Amend button brings up the standard Amend Contact screen.

Enter Contract / Advice Note

If contact identification has been used to identify the customer, then the Site and Contact fields are populated automatically by the system on the ‘Enter Hire Contract’ and ‘Enter Advice Note’ screens. 

The Contact field is also made read-only, as is the ‘Amend Contact Details’ screen if you double-click on the contact name.  The same is also true on the ‘Amend Hire Contract’ screen and the ‘Amend / Cancel Advice Note’ screens.  

Hence, the contact will be populated automatically and then cannot be changed from then on, for the entire lifetime of the contract or advice note.
