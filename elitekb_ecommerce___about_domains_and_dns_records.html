About Domains and DNS Records

The first thing to know is that Rental Hosting DOES NOT MANAGE YOUR DOMAIN OR ITS RENEWAL.

Managing your domain and being sure it is renewed is YOUR responsibility. Some store owners have a buddy with a company down the street who does techy stuff and takes care of all this for them. That’s ok, but just in case something happens – it’s a much better idea to manage this yourself or to pay a reputable IT company to do it for you.

Tips for managing your domain:
 

  • Using various online lookup tools such as https://lookup.icann.org/, https://www.whatsmyip.org/whois-dns-lookup/, etc. you can easily know exactly when your domain is going to expire. THIS IS EXTREMELY IMPORTANT. Why? Because domain “scammers” lurk about, waiting for a domain to expire so they can then purchase it and ransom it for thousands of $$$ back to the original domain owner. You don’t want this to happen to you!
     

  • Make sure you have your domain set to auto-renew.
     

  • Make sure you have a valid credit card on file for the renewals and that you update it when it’s set to expire.
     

  • Make sure the contact information (email address, phone number, etc.) in the account is valid so that you will receive domain renewal notifications from the domain registrar.
     

  • Renew your domain to the maximum number of years. Legitimate domain registrars should not charge more than ~$20/year for domain renewals.
     

  • Be aware. If you receive letters or emails from other “domain registration/renewal services” claiming they want to manage your domain’s renewal (often for $50 to $200/year), be very careful, as most of these are also scammers wanting to take over domains, etc. In short, JUST SAY NO.

 
 

So, if eCommerce doesn’t do this for you, what DO we do?

We host your integrated website. That means that the files that control your website’s look and function are stored on our server and the all-knowing Internet knows to look to our server for your domain’s files. How does it possibly know that?

Because your domain account has that information stored in it. That information is kept in Nameservers with entries called DNS records. (Think of Nameservers as a phone book and DNS records as listings in the phone book). These records can control various things, such as where to look for your website’s files and your email hosting information.

The DNS record is where the IP address to our server is stored. There should also be a DNS CNAME record redirecting www to the root domain.

These are the only two DNS records we are concerned with. Any other DNS records are your responsibility (or that of your IT folks’) – such as the MX records, which control your email hosting. eCommerce does not manage your email hosting.

Nameservers can be hosted in your domain account or in a separate account with a separate company. Using the same links mentioned above, you can find where your nameservers are hosted.
